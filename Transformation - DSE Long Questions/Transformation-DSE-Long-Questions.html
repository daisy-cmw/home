2015-Q18 (9 Marks)

Let \(f(x) = 2x^2 - 4kx + 3k^2 + 5\), where \(k\) is a real constant.
a) Does the graph of \(y = f(x)\) cut the \(x\)-axis? Explain your answer. [2 Marks]
Check the sign of the quadratic discriminant \(\Delta = b^2 - 4ac\).
Step 1.Set coefficients: \(a = 2\), \(b = -4k\), \(c = 3k^2 + 5\).
Step 2.Discriminant calculation: $$\Delta = (-4k)^2 - 4(2)(3k^2 + 5) = 16k^2 - 24k^2 - 40 = -8k^2 - 40$$
Step 3.Sign test: Since \(-8k^2 \le 0\) for all real \(k\), then \(-8k^2 - 40 \le -40 < 0\).
Step 4.Conclusion: Since \(\Delta < 0\), the graph never cuts the \(x\)-axis.
b) Using the method of completing the square, express, in terms of \(k\), the coordinates of the vertex of the graph of \(y = f(x)\). [3 Marks]
Factor out the leading scalar coefficient 2 before completing the perfect square square.
Step 1.Factorize 2 out: $$f(x) = 2(x^2 - 2kx) + 3k^2 + 5$$
Step 2.+ and - balance terms: $$f(x) = 2(x^2 - 2kx + k^2 - k^2) + 3k^2 + 5$$
Step 3.Simplify form: $$f(x) = 2(x - k)^2 - 2k^2 + 3k^2 + 5 = 2(x - k)^2 + k^2 + 5$$
Step 4.By vertex form: Vertex = \((k, k^2 + 5)\).
c) In the same rectangular coordinate system, let \(S\) and \(T\) be moving points on the graph of \(y = f(x)\) and the graph of \(y = 2 - f(x)\) respectively. Denote the origin by \(O\). Someone claims that when \(S\) and \(T\) are nearest to each other, the circumcentre of \(\Delta OST\) lies on the \(x\)-axis. Is the claim correct? Explain your answer. [4 Marks]
Graphs are reflections of each other. Find the midpoint and equation of the perpendicular bisector line.
Step 1.Points \(S\) and \(T\) are closest at their respective vertices. Vertex \(S = (k, k^2 + 5)\).
Step 2.Find profile of \(y = 2 - f(x)\): $$y = 2 - [2(x - k)^2 + k^2 + 5] = -2(x - k)^2 - k^2 - 3$$
Step 3.Vertex \(T = (k, -k^2 - 3)\). Line segment \(ST\) is purely vertical because both share \(x = k\).
Step 4.Midpoint of \(ST\): $$\text{Midpoint } y = \frac{(k^2 + 5) + (-k^2 - 3)}{2} = \frac{2}{2} = 1 \implies (k, 1)$$
Step 5.Perpendicular bisector equation: Horizontal line \(y = 1\).
Step 6.Conclusion: The circumcentre must lie on \(y = 1\), so it can never lie on the \(x\)-axis (\(y = 0\)). Claim is incorrect.

2016-Q18 (6 Marks)

Let \(f(x) = -\frac{1}{3}x^2 + 12x - 121\).
a) Using the method of completing the square, find the coordinates of the vertex of the graph of \(y = f(x)\). [2 Marks]
Factor out the fractional term -1/3 from the first two parts carefully.
Step 1.Factorize \(-\frac{1}{3}\) out: $$f(x) = -\frac{1}{3}(x^2 - 36x) - 121$$
Step 2.+ and - balance terms: $$f(x) = -\frac{1}{3}(x^2 - 36x + 18^2 - 18^2) - 121$$
Step 3.Simplify form: $$f(x) = -\frac{1}{3}(x - 18)^2 + 108 - 121 = -\frac{1}{3}(x - 18)^2 - 13$$
Step 4.By vertex form: Vertex = \((18, -13)\).
b) The graph of \(y = g(x)\) is obtained by translating the graph of \(y = f(x)\) vertically. If the graph of \(y = g(x)\) touches the \(x\)-axis, find \(g(x)\). [2 Marks]
To touch the x-axis, the vertex y-coordinate output must be brought directly up to 0.
Step 1.Target vertex format must become \((18, 0)\).
Step 2.Shift graph up 13 units to cancel out the current height of \(-13\).
Step 3.Result equation: \(g(x) = f(x) + 13 = -\frac{1}{3}(x - 18)^2\).
c) Under a transformation, \(f(x)\) is changed to \(-\frac{1}{3}x^2 - 12x - 121\). Describe the geometric meaning of the transformation. [2 Marks]
Evaluate what substitution rule gives this updated middle term sign change.
Step 1.Substitute \(x \to -x\) into the original function: $$f(-x) = -\frac{1}{3}(-x)^2 + 12(-x) - 121 = -\frac{1}{3}x^2 - 12x - 121$$
Step 2.Geometric meaning: Since internal \(x\) inverted signs, the graph is reflected about the \(y\)-axis.

2019-Q19 (12 Marks)

Let \(f(x) = \frac{1}{1+k}[x^2 + (6k-2)x + (9k+25)]\), where \(k\) is a positive constant. Denote the point \((4, 33)\) by \(F\).
a) Prove that the graph of \(y = f(x)\) passes through \(F\). [1 Mark]
Step 1.Substitute \(x = 4\) into the right side equation: $$f(4) = \frac{1}{1+k}[4^2 + (6k-2)(4) + 9k + 25]$$
Step 2.Expand value components inside: $$f(4) = \frac{1}{1+k}[16 + 24k - 8 + 9k + 25] = \frac{33k + 33}{1+k}$$
Step 3.Factor common factor out: \(\frac{33(k+1)}{1+k} = 33\).
Step 4.Conclusion: Since \(f(4) = 33\), the path passes through point \(F\).
b) The graph of \(y = g(x)\) is obtained by reflecting the graph of \(y = f(x)\) with respect to the \(y\)-axis and then translating the resulting graph upwards by 4 units. Let \(U\) be the vertex of the graph of \(y = g(x)\). Denote the origin by \(O\).
(i) Using the method of completing the square, express the coordinates of \(U\) in terms of \(k\).
Step 1.Apply functional rules: \(g(x) = f(-x) + 4\). $$g(x) = \frac{1}{1+k}[x^2 - (6k-2)x + 9k + 25] + 4$$
Step 2.+ and - balance terms internally: $$g(x) = \frac{1}{1+k}[(x - (3k-1))^2 - (3k-1)^2 + 9k + 25] + 4$$
Step 3.Expand internal constants and clean expression: $$g(x) = \frac{1}{1+k}[x - (3k-1)]^2 + \frac{-(9k^2 - 6k + 1) + 9k + 25 + 4(1+k)}{1+k}$$
Step 4.Factor fraction: \(\frac{-9k^2 + 19k + 28}{1+k} = \frac{(k+1)(28-9k)}{1+k} = 28 - 9k\).
Step 5.By vertex form: Vertex \(U = \mathbf{(3k - 1, 28 - 9k)}\).
(ii) Find \(k\) such that the area of the circle passing through \(F\), \(O\) and \(U\) is the least.
Step 1.Circle area is minimized when segment \(OF\) forms the direct diameter line.
Step 2.By Thales semicircle theorem, this forces a right angle alignment: \(\angle OUF = 90^\circ\).
Step 3.Perpendicular slope condition: \(m_{FU} \times m_{OU} = -1\). $$\left(\frac{28-9k}{3k-1}\right) \times \left(\frac{33 - (28-9k)}{4 - (3k-1)}\right) = -1$$
Step 4.Simplify to standard form quadratic: \(2k^2 - 5k - 3 = 0\).
Step 5.Solve factorization: \((2k+1)(k-3) = 0 \implies \mathbf{k = 3}\) (reject negative root since \(k>0\)).
(iii) For any positive constant \(k\), the graph of \(y = g(x)\) passes through the same point \(G\). Let \(V\) be the vertex of the graph of \(y = g(x)\) such that the area of the circle passing through \(F\), \(O\) and \(V\) is the least. Are \(F\), \(G\), \(O\) and \(V\) concyclic? Explain your answer.
Step 1.Find point \(G\) by transforming \(F(4,33)\): Reflect \(x \to -4\), shift \(y \to 33+4=37 \implies G(-4, 37)\).
Step 2.Evaluate vertex \(V\) coordinates at \(k=3\): \(V = (3(3)-1, 28-9(3)) = (8, 1)\).
Step 3.Test if \(\angle FGO = 90^\circ\) via gradient calculation: $$m_{FG} \times m_{OG} = \left(\frac{37-33}{-4-4}\right) \times \left(\frac{37-0}{-4-0}\right) = \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) \times \left(-\frac{37}{4}\right) = \frac{37}{8}$$
Step 4.Conclusion: Since the product \(\frac{37}{8} \neq -1\), \(\angle FGO \neq 90^\circ\). Points are not concyclic.

2024-Q19 (6 Marks)

Let \(f(x) = 2x^2 + 4mx + 8x + 2m^2 + 8m + n\), where \(m\) and \(n\) are real constants such that \(mn < 0\). Denote the vertex of the graph of \(y = f(x)\) by \(P\).
a) Using the method of completing the square, express, in terms of \(m\) and \(n\), the coordinates of the vertex \(P\) of the graph of \(y = f(x)\). [4 Marks]
Group the linear \(x\) terms as \(4(m+2)x\) and extract the leading coefficient 2 before completing the perfect square.
Step 1.Group \(x\) terms and factor out 2: $$f(x) = 2[x^2 + 2(m + 2)x] + 2m^2 + 8m + n$$
Step 2.Add and subtract the square of half the \(x\)-coefficient inside the bracket: $$f(x) = 2[x^2 + 2(m + 2)x + (m + 2)^2 - (m + 2)^2] + 2m^2 + 8m + n$$
Step 3.Form the perfect square and expand the trailing constant part: $$f(x) = 2(x + m + 2)^2 - 2(m^2 + 4m + 4) + 2m^2 + 8m + n$$
Step 4.Simplify the expression: $$f(x) = 2(x + m + 2)^2 - 2m^2 - 8m - 8 + 2m^2 + 8m + n = 2(x + (m + 2))^2 + n - 8$$
Step 5.Determine the vertex form coordinates: Vertex \(P = \mathbf{(-m - 2, n - 8)}\).
b) Describe the geometric meaning represented by transforming \(f(x)\) to \(f\left(\frac{x}{5}\right) + 7\). [2 Marks]
Step 1.Horizontal transformation: Input fraction \(\frac{x}{5}\) means enlarge 5 times along the \(x\)-axis (horizontally).
Step 2.Vertical transformation: Trailing scalar addition \(+7\) means shift 7 units upward (vertically).

2025-Q18 (9 Marks)

Let \(g(x) = 3x^2 - 6kx + 24x + 3k^2 - 24k + 55\), where \(k\) is a positive constant. Denote the vertex of the graph of \(y = g(x)\) by \(R\).
a) Using the method of completing the square, express the coordinates of \(R\) in terms of \(k\). [2 Marks]
Step 1.Factorize 3 out of variable components: $$g(x) = 3[x^2 - 2(k-4)x] + 3k^2 - 24k + 55$$
Step 2.+ and - balance terms internally: $$g(x) = 3[(x - (k-4))^2 - (k-4)^2] + 3k^2 - 24k + 55$$
Step 3.Expand and simplify remaining constants: $$g(x) = 3(x - (k-4))^2 - 3(k^2 - 8k + 16) + 3k^2 - 24k + 55 = 3(x - (k-4))^2 + 7$$
Step 4.By vertex form: Coordinate \(R = \mathbf{(k - 4, 7)}\).
b) The graph of \(y = h(x)\) is obtained by translating the graph of \(y = g(x)\) rightwards by 6 units and then downwards by 10 units. Denote the vertex of the graph of \(y = h(x)\) by \(S\). Let \(T\) be a point such that the orthocentre of \(\Delta RST\) is the origin.
(i) Express the coordinates of \(T\) in terms of \(k\).
Step 1.Find shifted vertex coordinate position \(S\): $$S = (k - 4 + 6, 7 - 10) = (k + 2, -3)$$
Step 2.Let target position be \(T(a,b)\). Since \(O\) is the orthocentre, altitude lines must satisfy perpendicular properties.
Step 3.Apply condition \(OT \perp RS \implies m_{OT} \times m_{RS} = -1\): $$\frac{b}{a} \times \frac{-3-7}{(k+2)-(k-4)} = -1 \implies \frac{b}{a} \times \left(-\frac{10}{6}\right) = -1 \implies b = 0.6a$$
Step 4.Apply condition \(OS \perp TR \implies m_{OS} \times m_{TR} = -1\): $$\left(\frac{-3}{k+2}\right) \times \left(\frac{7-b}{k-4-a}\right) = -1 \implies 3(b-7) = (k+2)(a-k+4)$$
Step 5.Substitute linear substitution value \(b = 0.6a\) and expand equation terms to solve for parameter \(a\): $$1.8a - 21 = (k+2)a - k^2 + 2k + 8 \implies a = \frac{k^2 - 2k - 29}{k + 0.2}$$
Step 6.Result coordinate vector format: \(T\left(\frac{k^2 - 2k - 29}{k + 0.2}, \frac{0.6(k^2 - 2k - 29)}{k + 0.2}\right)\).
(ii) The coordinates of the point \(U\) are \((-5, 5)\). Let \(V\) be a point lying on \(RS\) such that \(TV\) is perpendicular to \(RS\). It is given that \(S\), \(T\), \(U\) and \(V\) are concyclic. Find \(k\).
Step 1.Since \(\angle TVS = 90^\circ\), concyclic points sharing segment \(TS\) mean \(\angle TUS = 90^\circ\) too.
Step 2.Perpendicular condition: \(m_{TU} \times m_{US} = -1\): $$\left(\frac{b-5}{a+5}\right) \times \left(\frac{-3-5}{k+2+5}\right) = -1 \implies 8(b-5) = (a+5)(k+7)$$
Step 3.Substitute \(b = 0.6a\) to get alternative equation profile for variable \(a\): $$4.8a - 40 = ak + 7a + 5k + 35 \implies a = \frac{5k+75}{-k-2.2}$$
Step 4.Equate both found expressions for \(a\): $$\frac{k^2 - 2k - 29}{k + 0.2} = \frac{5k + 75}{-k - 2.2} \implies k^3 - 5.2k^2 + 42.6k - 48.8 = 0$$
Step 5.Factorize cubic form polynomial: \((k - 1)(k^2 - 4.2k + 38.4) = 0\).
Step 6.Since quadratic part has no real roots (\(\Delta < 0\)), single root is \(k = 1\). Claim is correct.
